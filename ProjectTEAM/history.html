History of Pokémon

Pokémon is a media franchise created by Satoshi Tajiri and developed by Nintendo, Game Freak, and Creatures. It began in 1996 in Japan with the release of Pokémon Red and Green for the Game Boy. The concept was inspired by Tajiri’s childhood interest in collecting creatures and his love for nature.

The franchise centers around trainers capturing, training, and battling creatures called Pokémon. Each Pokémon has unique abilities, types, and evolutions, encouraging strategy and collection. The slogan “Gotta Catch ’Em All!” became iconic worldwide.

After the original games, Pokémon expanded rapidly into video games, trading cards, an animated TV series, movies, toys, and merchandise, becoming one of the most successful entertainment franchises in history. By 2025, there are over 1,100 Pokémon species across multiple game generations. Pokémon also influenced popular culture globally, fostering a massive fan community, competitive gaming, and innovations like Pokémon Go, the augmented reality mobile game released in 2016.

Pokémon Then

Pokémon Then: Started in 1996 with Red & Blue. 151 Pokémon. 2D graphics. Goal: catch all Pokémon and become champion. Anime: Ash travels with Pikachu to become a Pokémon Master. Simple adventures, “monster-of-the-week.” Cards & Toys: Pokémon cards, toys, and clothes became very popular. Culture: Big trend. “Gotta catch ’em all!” Pokémon was fun and easy for everyone.

Pokémon Now

Pokémon Now: Games: Over 1000 Pokémon, 3D worlds, online battles, open-world games, mobile AR games (Pokémon GO). Anime: Still going! More complex stories. Ash finally won a league in 2021. Cards & Toys: Cards are very collectible and can be expensive. Merchandise is everywhere. Culture: Loved by kids and adults. People play online, compete, and create fan content.
